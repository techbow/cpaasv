Why, he at once requested all who had been members of the old Parliament, of any regular Parliament in the time of Charles the Second, to assemble.

The Peers, being a standing body, could of course assemble; and all they did was to recommend the calling of a convention, to be chosen by the same electors, and composed of the same numbers, as composed a Parliament.

The convention assembled, and, as all know, was turned into a Parliament. Not merely because a new sovereign then ascended the throne of the Stuarts, but because there was a change in the organization of the government.
